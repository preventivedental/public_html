First Visit Routine Expectations

This visit is focused on giving you a clear picture of your mouth. Every person is different and I do not know what type of cleaning you need until I look at your gums, bone, buildup, and X rays. The goal is to make this visit productive and worthwhile.

What I Look For

I check how long it has been since your last cleaning, how much buildup is present, if there is bleeding or swelling, and if pockets are present around the teeth. I look at whether those pockets are active or stable. I also check for recession, bone loss, and any signs of infection. What you do at home plays a major role, so I look at how healthy the gums are based on your daily habits.

What This Means for Your Cleaning

A routine cleaning is only for healthy gums with light buildup. If the gums are bleeding, infected, or the pockets are deep, a different level of care is needed. I choose the correct procedure based on what I find. It does not help you if I force a routine cleaning on a mouth that needs deeper work.

Examples

  • Light buildup with healthy gums qualifies for a routine cleaning.
  • Moderate or heavy buildup needs more time and deeper therapy.
  • Deep or bleeding pockets require treatment that targets the infected areas.

Understanding Costs

The type of cleaning you need is based on your gums and the condition of your mouth. Routine cleanings and deeper treatments have different fees, and, if you have insurance, it helps with some parts more than others. I will tell you what you're eligible for and what your plan is expected to pay before anything is done, so you always know your share up front. There are no surprises and no pressure. My goal is to match the right treatment to your mouth and keep both the care and the costs clear and simple.

What You Can Do to Help

Tell me how long it has been since your last cleaning. Tell me if things bleed when you brush or if anything feels tender or sensitive. Tell me if you know you have buildup. Being honest helps me prepare and makes your visit smoother.

Direct Contact

If you have questions before or after your visit, you can contact me directly. Use my contact card to call, text, or email me privately. Your message goes straight to me. This keeps everything simple and avoids phone tag or missed information. You can send updates, concerns, or photos if needed.
