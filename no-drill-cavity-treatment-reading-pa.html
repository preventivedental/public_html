What Is SMART — and Why Do We Offer It?

SMART stands for Silver Modified Atraumatic Restorative Technique — a long name for a simple idea: stop decay gently, without drilling or numbing.

It’s not new, and it’s not a shortcut. SMART has been used worldwide, especially for patients who can’t tolerate conventional treatment. It combines silver diamine fluoride (to stop the bacteria) with a special glass filling (to seal the tooth).

We offer it for kids, seniors, patients with anxiety, or anyone looking for a lower-intervention approach. It’s not for every cavity — but for the right one, it works beautifully.

Here’s What No-Drill Really Means

Let’s be real — sometimes we do need to clean things out a bit before sealing it. But it’s nothing like the traditional drill experience.

Think of it more like a power toothbrush: gentle polishing to clear out the bad stuff. No shots. No numb face. Most people say, “That’s it?”

Then we use the SMART method to stop bacteria and protect the tooth with a clean, sealed layer. You’ll stay totally in control the entire time.

If at any point you’re not comfortable, we pause. Simple as that.

When SMART Isn’t the Right Fit

SMART is a great option — but it’s not for every situation. Some teeth need more structure, deeper treatment, or a stronger restoration than this method allows.

If we see something that needs more than what SMART can do, we’ll explain exactly what’s going on, why, and what your options are.

No pressure. No upsell. No scare tactics. Just a calm, honest conversation about what’s best for your tooth — and for you.

Not Ready to Come In Yet? Try This First.

The most important thing: keep things clean.

  • Brush with a high-fluoride toothpaste — it can help strengthen early decay
  • Cut back on frequent sugar snacks (it feeds cavity bacteria)
  • Try xylitol gum — it helps reduce cavity-causing bacteria
  • Spit, don’t rinse after brushing — this keeps fluoride on your teeth longer

These tips can help slow things down — but if you’re already feeling discomfort or seeing discoloration, that’s your cue to let us take a look.
