Temporary Crown or Bridge Care What to expect, what to avoid, and what happens next

What to Expect

You’ve got a temporary in place right now. It’s just a placeholder while the real thing is being made at an outside lab. I don’t control how fast or slow they move, but I’ll stay on top of it and get you scheduled as soon as it’s ready. I've had some come in a day or two, and as long as two weeks. Each case is unique.

Some sensitivity is normal for a few days. Your bite might feel off. That’s expected and will be adjusted when the final one is placed.

How to Take Care of the Temporary

  • Brush normally, but be gentle around the temporary.
  • Floss carefully. Slide the floss out. Don’t pull upward.
  • If it comes off, save it and contact us. We can re-cement it quickly.

What to Avoid

  • No sticky stuff like gum or taffy. It can pull the temporary off.
  • Skip hard foods like ice, nuts, or hard pretzels on that side.
  • Don’t mess with the temporary or test it. Just let it be.

What Happens Next

Your permanent crown or bridge is in the works. Once it’s back from the lab, we’ll contact you to schedule the seat appointment. If it’s been two weeks and you haven’t heard from us, feel free to check in. Sometimes labs take longer than they should. I don’t like it either, but I’ll make sure you’re not forgotten.

Final Thought

This is just a step in the process. You’re not on your own. If anything feels off or something breaks, reach out. I’m here to make sure the end result fits well and feels right.
