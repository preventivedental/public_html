After Your Tooth Extraction

What to expect, how to heal well, and when to call us.

What to Expect

It's normal to have some bleeding, swelling, and sorness after your extraction. Healing usually takes a few days. You're got this! And we've got your back if anything feels of.

🩸 Bleeding & Gauze

Bite firmly on gauze for 30–60 minutes. A little oozing is normal. If bleeding continues, swap for a new piece and bite again.

💊 Pain & Meds

Take ibuprofen or Tylenol as directed. Avoid aspirin. Pain usually peaks on day 2–3 and improves after that.

🍲 Eating & Healing ✨

Soft, cool foods today. No straws, smoking, or vigorous rinsing for 24–48 hours. Let that clot stay protected.

🌟 You’re Doing Great

This isn’t fun — we know. But your health is worth it. Every hour that passes, your body is healing. Stay hydrated, rest, and reach out if anything feels off.

🚨 Call Us If You Notice...

  • Bleeding that doesn’t stop after 2 hours
  • Severe pain that medication doesn’t help
  • Fever, chills, or nausea
  • Swelling that gets worse after 48 hours
  • Bad taste or smell in your mouth

We’ve got your back. Call or text any time.

🙅 What NOT To Do

  • ❌ No drinking through straws
  • ❌ Don’t smoke or vape
  • ❌ Avoid spicy, crunchy, or hot foods
  • ❌ Don’t touch the area with your tongue
  • ❌ Don’t skip meals (your body needs fuel to heal)
