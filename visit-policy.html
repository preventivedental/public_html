Appointment and Financial Terms

We keep care straightforward and efficient. This page explains how we handle appointments, payments, and communication so your visit goes smoothly.

Appointments

When we make an appointment, it's confirmed. We set that time aside for you. Any reminders you get from us are a courtesy, not a re-confirmation.

Please give at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule.

Missed or same-day cancellations may carry a fee depending on the situation. If you arrive more than fifteen minutes late, we may need to move your appointment later in the day or to another available time so we can stay on schedule for other patients. Fees may range from $35 to $100 depending on the time reserved and the type of appointment.

Repeated short-notice cancellations or no-shows may affect future scheduling.

Insurance Questions

Every plan works differently. If you'd like to use your insurance, simply tell us the name of your plan and we'll explain how we work with it before your visit.

We'll make sure you know what to expect ahead of time, including any estimated costs or copays, so there are no surprises.

Financial Responsibility

Payment is due at the time of service unless prior arrangements have been made. We provide clear estimates before treatment so you can make informed decisions.

Any balance not cleared within 30 days may be subject to a monthly finance charge of up to 1.5% (18% annually) or a minimum $10 late fee. Returned checks or declined payments include a $35 processing fee. Accounts unpaid after reasonable notice may be referred for outside collection or filed with the local magistrate's office or small claims court for recovery. Outstanding balances may need to be resolved before additional non-urgent appointments can be scheduled.

Appointment and Payment Readiness

Every appointment is connected to payment readiness. An appointment is confirmed when we have a verified way to handle payment either through confirmed insurance coverage or direct payment arrangements.

If insurance is being used, we verify that the plan is active and that benefits are available before the visit. If we find that coverage has ended, benefits have been used elsewhere, or key services are no longer covered, we’ll reach out to correct this before the appointment.

If we don’t hear back or can’t confirm how the visit will be paid for within a reasonable time, we may release that appointment to another patient. We reserve the right to adjust or remove appointments that cannot be confirmed financially.

Emergencies and Same-Day Visits

Emergency appointments are worked into the schedule as time allows. While we do our best to help quickly, we can’t always accommodate specific times on short notice.

If more than one issue comes up, we start with the area causing pain or infection and schedule follow-up visits as needed.

Minors and Dependents

The parent or legal guardian who brings the child to the appointment is financially responsible for payment at the time of service, regardless of custody arrangements, court orders, or third-party expectations. We do not bill non-attending parents, guardians, or outside parties. Any reimbursement between parents or guardians must be handled privately.

Communication

Texting is the fastest and most reliable way to confirm or adjust appointments. We also respond by email for non-urgent matters that require documentation. Written communication helps maintain clear records and ensures nothing is missed.
