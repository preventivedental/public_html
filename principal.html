PRINCIPAL PATIENT NOTICE

We’re no longer in-network with Principal as of November 12, 2025.

Principal has entered an arrangement that allows other companies to access its PPO network rates. This means Principal leased out its reduced fee schedule, giving other carriers access to the same low reimbursements even though those carriers had been paying my office higher rates before this arrangement.

This change would result in a pay cut for me and my team.

Rather than continue under a setup that forces lower pay for the same care, I’ve chosen to end participation with Principal’s PPO network before this goes into effect.

Nothing about your care changes. You can still be seen here, and my team will continue to help you file your claims. The only difference is that Principal will now pay you directly instead of paying the office.

Here’s what Principal has paid me over time:

Procedure Code 2011 Principal 2025
Routine Check-Up D0120 $27 $33
Adult Cleaning D1110 $55 $62
Two-Surface Resin Filling D2392 $126 $142
Panoramic Image D0330 $71 $81

Average increase: about $10–15 over 14 years.
Inflation and cost increases over that same time: more than 40%.

In other words, Principal’s reimbursements haven’t kept up with reality. When they leased these same rates out to other insurers, it meant lower pay across the board for the same work.

Here’s why this matters:

  • Principal’s reimbursement rates were already low.
  • By leasing those rates, other insurers can now pay less for the same care.
  • This move effectively created a pay cut without any notice to providers.

I made this decision to protect the quality of your care and to ensure I can continue spending the time and resources necessary to do things the right way. Insurance companies can cut corners. I won’t.

If you have Principal coverage, you’re still welcome here. You’ll simply pay at the time of service and receive reimbursement directly from your plan.

This change was necessary. It’s the only way to maintain honest, high-quality dentistry without letting insurers dictate what your care is worth.
